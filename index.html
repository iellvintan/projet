Brainstorming

Group discussion to identify the problem space related to educational technology. Topics discussed:

  • Trouble Picking Courses
  • Izone Class Enrollment
  • Campus Parking System
  • Educational Forum
  • Tutor Tutee Pairing System
  • Mobile Project Management System
Campus Parking System received an overwhelming reaction from groupmates as everyone is facing difficulties in securing parking lot in Sunway University Campus. However, this idea does not particularly match the theme of “Educational Technology”. Trouble Picking Courses is a symptom instead of the underlying problem. The underlying problem could be teenagers do not understand themselves well enough to decide on which course to select. The higher education consultancy website provide plenty of info and career test helps the student to understand their strength and weakness. Izone class enrollment issues will not be a significant problem as students only required to enrol for the subjects each semester. The last option, Mobile Project Management System has been chosen by researching and applying logical elimination.

Define Problem

Undergraduates are facing numerous challenges when working on group projects or group assignments. The challenges included coordination costs, motivation costs, and intellectual costs.

Coordination Costs

Represent the time and energy that group work consumes, such as to coordinate schedules, arrange meetings, meet, correspond, and make decisions collectively. Most of the time, group tasks are highly interdependent in which all group members have to work accordingly to ensure the work is well done.

Motivation Costs

Refer to the negative effect on student motivation of working in a group, which often caused by these circumstances. These phenomena are free-riding, social loafing and conflict. Freeriding occurs when an individual leaves most or all of the work to the other group members while social loafing is the tendency of a person exerting less effort than he or she can.

Intellectual Costs

Describe the characteristics of group behaviour that can reduce creativity and productivity. Intellectual costs include groupthink and escalation of commitment. Groupthink is the tendency of group members to conform to a perceived majority view. Escalation of commitment is the tendency of ones to overcommit to his or her ineffective plans.

Analyze Ideas

We planned to target the coordination costs, reduce it and provide a better and easier way to collaborate. The idea has undergone PACT Analysis to verify and analyze whether it is suitable for our study. PACT analysis also acts as our brief user requirements document for the system.

PACT Analysis

People Our target users are undergraduate students aged between 18-25 who own a smartphone or have experience using one. User should have at least beginner or intermediate skill of using a mobile application. Our application focuses on 'see and click' walkthrough as people are better at recognizing things than memorizing them. User should be physically healthy, free from visual impairment and colour blindness. User should be able to read English or at least have elementary knowledge in English study.
Activities To lower the coordination costs, one of the main activity that is available for users is the Gantt progress chart. Each user can track the progress of other users while the project owner can assign tasks to everyone. Different colours are used to separate and differentiate different user's tasks. The users may click on their parts to update and complete to contribute to the overall progress. They can also click on other user's parts to view and proofread the details so that they can notify them if any mistake found. A tick symbol next to the parts shows that the tasks completed. There will be a build-in calendar allowing users to have an explicit visual approach on the days left to the deadline of the assignment. The calendar also helps the user to prioritize the tasks and organize meetings. When someone updates and completes their task, everyone in the group will receive a notification. Besides that, when deadlines and meetings are approaching, the system will notify everyone.
Contexts The application should be able to use in any places with internet access. It will work offline as well to view items without update for those who have slow internet access. It should fit every mobile screen size with different resolutions and colour depths. Our system caters for mobile device user as a smartphone has been the closest items to human nowadays. We want to help users reminding, updating, connecting them using the fastest route, which is the smartphone. The application is syncing 24/7 with internet access.
Technologies An android phone with at least an android version of 5.0 (Lollipop) or an iPhone with a minimum iOS version of 9.0 is required. Google Play Store or Apple Apps Store is required for the android phone or the iPhone, respectively. A working display or screen with a minimum size of 4 inches is the need for output.

Usability Inspection

Cognitive walkthrough is performed to understand the system's learnability for new users. The walkthrough could help us understand how to improve the animation and the flow between the activities.

Cognitive walkthrough

Materials Needed

  • Printed sketches representing the user interface
  • A task list that includes all the tasks needed in the walkthrough
  • A feedback form to receive responses and comments from the tester

People Involded

  • Facilitator: The organizer of the walkthrough and is responsible for making sure that the entire flow can run smoothly.
  • Evaluator: The tester for the system.
  • Notetaker: The one who records the output of the cognitive walkthrough.

Procedure

  1. Define the uses of the product and conduct a context of use analysis.
  2. Determine the most appropriate task for the walkthrough.
  3. Set some rules for the walkthrough, such as no interactions and no communications during the testing. The evaluators do not allow to use their phone or any devices during the walkthrough to prevent distractions.
  4. Conduct the walkthrough:
    • Present the representation of the system interface to the evaluator.
    • Walkthrough the action sequences for each task.
    • Record success stories, failure stories, design suggestions and problems
  5. Compile all the analysis and discussion among the team.
